<p><p>Cement additives can be classified into three main types: Chemical, Mineral, and Fibre. Chemical additives are substances used to enhance specific properties of cement, such as setting time and workability. Mineral additives are materials like fly ash or slag that are added to improve the overall strength and durability of the cement. Fibre additives, such as synthetic or natural fibers, are used to increase the tensile strength and prevent cracking in the final concrete product. Each type of additive plays a unique role in optimizing the performance of cement in construction applications.</p></p>
