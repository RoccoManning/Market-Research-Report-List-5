<p><ul><li>Non-hormonal</li><li>Hormonal</li><li>Inert</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCD) come in different types based on their composition and mechanism of action. Non-hormonal IUCDs work by creating a hostile environment for sperm, preventing fertilization. Hormonal IUCDs release hormones to prevent ovulation and thicken cervical mucus, making it difficult for sperm to reach the egg. Inert IUCDs are made of materials like copper that interfere with sperm motility and viability. These different types offer women a range of options for long-lasting and highly effective birth control.</p></p>
