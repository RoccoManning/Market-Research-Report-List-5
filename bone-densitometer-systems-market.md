<p><p>Bone densitometer systems are used to measure bone density and assess the risk of fractures in patients. There are two main types of bone densitometry systems: axial bone densitometry and peripheral bone densitometry. Axial bone densitometry involves scanning the hip and spine to measure bone mineral density, while peripheral bone densitometry measures bone density in the limbs, such as the wrist or heel. Both types of systems are essential for diagnosing osteoporosis and monitoring treatment effectiveness in patients at risk of fractures.</p></p>
