<p><p>Electric Vehicles (EVs) are categorized into two main market types: Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs). BEVs run solely on electricity from a charged battery, while PHEVs have a combination of battery power and an internal combustion engine. BEVs are emission-free and require charging stations for longer trips, while PHEVs offer greater flexibility with the option to switch to gasoline when needed. Both types are gaining popularity as environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional gasoline-powered vehicles.</p></p>
