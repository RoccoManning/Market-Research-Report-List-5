<p><p>Artificial blood products such as Perfluorocarbon-based Oxygen Carriers (PFBOCs) and Hemoglobin-based Oxygen Carriers (HBOCs) are two types of blood substitutes used in medical settings. PFBOCs work by dissolving and carrying high amounts of oxygen, while HBOCs utilize purified hemoglobin to transport oxygen in the bloodstream. Both products aim to provide oxygen to tissues in cases where traditional blood transfusions are not possible or practical. The market for these artificial blood products continues to grow as advancements in medical technology drive the demand for alternative options in blood therapy.</p></p>
