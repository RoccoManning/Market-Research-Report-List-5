<p><ul><li>Direct Vapor Deposition</li><li>Physical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Plasma Spraying</li><li>Spray Assisted Vapor Deposition</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nitride ceramic coatings are applied using various methods such as Direct Vapor Deposition, Physical Vapor Deposition, Plasma Spraying, and Spray Assisted Vapor Deposition. Direct Vapor Deposition involves the generation of a vapor phase of the coating material for deposition. Physical Vapor Deposition uses a vacuum process to deposit thin films. Plasma Spraying involves the melting of powdered coating material and spraying it onto the substrate. Spray Assisted Vapor Deposition combines vapor phase and liquid phase deposition. Other methods may include chemical vapor deposition or sol-gel techniques.</p></p>
