<p><p>RF/Microwave mixers are essential components in communication systems, used to convert frequencies, modulate signals, and perform frequency upconversion or downconversion. There are two main types of mixers: Active mixers utilize transistors to amplify and mix signals, while Passive mixers use diode-based circuits for signal mixing. Active mixers offer better performance in terms of noise figure and linearity but have higher power consumption, while passive mixers are simpler and more cost-effective. Both types play crucial roles in various RF and microwave applications.</p></p>
