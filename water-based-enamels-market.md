<p><p>Water-based enamels are primarily classified into three types: polyurethane, styrene butadiene, and acrylic. Polyurethane enamels exhibit excellent durability and resistance to wear and tear, making them suitable for high-traffic areas. Styrene butadiene enamels offer good adhesion and flexibility, making them ideal for use on surfaces that undergo frequent movement. Acrylic enamels are known for their fast drying time and excellent color retention properties, making them a popular choice for interior and exterior applications. Each type of enamel has unique characteristics that cater to specific needs in the market.</p></p>
