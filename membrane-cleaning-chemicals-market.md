<p><ul><li>Caustic</li><li>Oxidants or Disinfectants</li><li>Acids</li><li>Surfactants</li><li>Chelating Agents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Membrane cleaning chemicals are categorized into different types based on their properties and functions. This includes caustic chemicals, oxidants or disinfectants, acids, surfactants, and chelating agents. Caustic chemicals are used to remove organic fouling, while oxidants or disinfectants are effective against microbial growth. Acids are used to dissolve mineral scaling, surfactants help in removing oily substances, and chelating agents bind with metal ions to prevent scaling. Each type of cleaning chemical plays a specific role in maintaining the efficiency of membrane systems.</p></p>
