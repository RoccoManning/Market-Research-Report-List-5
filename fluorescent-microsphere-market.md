<p><p>Fluorescent microspheres can be classified into two main types: hollow microspheres and solid microspheres. Hollow microspheres are typically made of a shell-like material that surrounds a void space, while solid microspheres are composed of a dense, uniform material throughout. Both types of microspheres are designed to emit fluorescent light when exposed to certain wavelengths of light, making them valuable tools for various applications such as biomedical imaging, flow cytometry, and particle tracking. Each type offers unique advantages and can be tailored to specific research needs.</p></p>
