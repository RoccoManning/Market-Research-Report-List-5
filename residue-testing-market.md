<p><p>Residue testing is conducted using various methods such as chromatography, mass spectroscopy, ELISA, PCR, and other specialized techniques. Chromatography separates compounds for analysis, while mass spectroscopy identifies and quantifies substances based on their molecular weight. ELISA tests use antibodies to detect residues, PCR amplifies and analyzes DNA fragments, and other methods include immunoassays and microbiological tests. Each method has its own advantages and applications in testing for residues in agricultural products, pharmaceuticals, and environmental samples.</p></p>
