<p><p>Self-lubricated bearings are divided into two main types: Metallic and Non-Metallic. Metallic self-lubricated bearings are made from materials like bronze or steel, which contain solid lubricants that reduce friction and wear. Non-Metallic self-lubricated bearings, on the other hand, are made from materials like plastic or composite materials that have embedded lubricants. Both types offer low maintenance and durability, making them ideal for applications where traditional lubrication methods are not feasible or desirable.</p></p>
